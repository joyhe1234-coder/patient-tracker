Dropdown Cell UX Prototypes

Compare 5 designs for making AG Grid dropdown cells more discoverable. Click cells to interact.

Current Problem

Users must double-click a dropdown cell to enter edit mode, then click again to open the dropdown list. That's 3 clicks for a single selection. There's no visual hint that a cell contains a dropdown.

Design A: Chevron Indicator + Single Click

Recommended

A small down-arrow chevron is always visible on dropdown cells (faded when idle, solid on hover). Single click opens the dropdown immediately. Non-dropdown cells still use double-click.

Design B: Hover-Reveal Button

Minimal

Cell looks completely normal at rest. On hover, a small blue dropdown button appears on the right side. Clicking the button (or the cell) opens the dropdown in one click.

Design C: Always-Visible Select Box

Most Obvious

Dropdown cells are styled as a bordered select/input field with a permanent down-arrow, like a standard HTML select. Single click opens options. It's immediately obvious which cells are dropdowns.

Design D: Inline Expand (Accordion-Style)

Unique

Single click on a dropdown cell expands the options directly below the cell as an inline list, no popup needed. Click an option to select, or click outside to close. Feels like expanding a section.

Design E: Underline Hint + Single Click Popup

Balanced

Dropdown cell text has a subtle dashed underline (like a link hint), signaling it's interactive. Single click opens a popup dropdown. The underline disappears during editing.
